Vibe-Math Your Way to a 60-Year-Old Breakthrough

Source ↗ Tools & Platforms

What This Skill Is

An amateur mathematician used ChatGPT to solve a 60-year-old Erdős problem that had stumped specialists for decades. Per Scientific American, experts said the proof method "had not occurred to humans" before.

The technique works for any hard problem where you have intuition but no credentialed expertise. The key: ask the model to attack the problem from every angle, including obscure ones — then you verify.

Why It Works

The amateur brought taste and verification; ChatGPT brought breadth. The proof was only possible because the model could try methods the human wouldn't have considered. The division of labor — human judgment + AI exploration — is the skill.

How to Use It

  1. State your problem with journal-level precision — not how you'd explain it to a classmate
  2. Let the model enumerate every method, including obscure ones
  3. Push it to attack from each angle and report failures as well as successes
  4. When something looks promising, formalize step by step
  5. Verify the math yourself — the model is your collaborator, not your proof checker

Prompt Template

You are a research collaborator. I have a hard problem: [STATE THE PROBLEM PRECISELY IN ONE PARAGRAPH].

First, list every method or framework that might apply, including unusual ones. Don't filter for "the obvious approach."

Then attempt the problem with each method. After each attempt, report:
- What worked
- What failed
- What new insight (if any) the failure produced

When you find a promising direction, formalize the argument step by step. I will verify each step independently.
