Use "String Seed of Thought" to Get More Diverse, Less Biased Answers

Source ↗ Tools & Platforms

What This Skill Is

LLMs are bad at randomness. Ask one to "pick a number between 1 and 100" a hundred times and you'll see 42 and 37 way more than chance. That bias warps brainstorming, A/B selection, and synthetic-data generation.

Sakana AI's SSoT (String Seed of Thought) fix: have the model generate a random string first, then deterministically manipulate that string to derive its answer. The manipulation step forces outputs away from the model's built-in preferences.

Why It Works

The ASCII manipulation step creates a deterministic but unpredictable transformation that bypasses the model's token preference biases. It's essentially forcing the model to roll a die before it thinks.

Then sum the ASCII values of the characters modulo [N], where N is the number of options I gave you. Use that number as your index (0-based) into the options. Do this BEFORE you reason about which option you prefer.
Report the string, the sum, and your selection.
