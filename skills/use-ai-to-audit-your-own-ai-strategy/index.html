Use AI to Audit Your Own AI Strategy

Source ↗ Tools & Platforms

What This Skill Is

Companies in the same industry with the same risks are living in completely different AI realities. One has been using enterprise ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini for 18 months. The one next door has a committee that individually approves every use case.

The deciding factor is whether a senior leader is willing to assume risk. This prompt gives leadership the information they need to make an actual decision — instead of defaulting to "no."

Why It Works

Most AI adoption blockers are legal/compliance teams protecting themselves from ambiguous risk. A structured audit separates real risks (with mitigations) from vague concerns — and quantifies the cost of staying still, which is often invisible until someone makes it explicit.

How to Use It

  1. Fill in your company type, size, and current AI policy (or describe it)
  2. Add what you know about competitors' AI use
  3. Run in Claude or ChatGPT with web search enabled
  4. Use the memo output as a starting point for the leadership conversation

Prompt Template

You are an AI adoption auditor. I work at a [company type] with [X employees]. Our current AI policy is: [paste policy or describe it]. Our competitors in this industry are using [tools you've heard about].

Please:
1. Identify which of our current restrictions are based on outdated assumptions vs. legitimate risks
2. List 3 peer companies in regulated industries (finance, healthcare, legal) that have deployed enterprise AI without incident — use web search for the most recent, credible examples
3. Draft a one-page memo I can send to leadership that separates real risks from FUD, with specific mitigations for each real risk
4. Include a "cost of inaction" section estimating what we lose per month by not deploying
