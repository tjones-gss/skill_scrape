Make a Permission Map Before Connecting AI to Anything Sensitive

Source ↗ Tools & Platforms

What This Skill Is

Before you connect an AI tool to sensitive data — email, bank accounts, files, CRM, calendar — make it write you a permission receipt. A permission map is a checklist of what the AI can see, what it can change, what it stores, and how you disconnect it.

The key distinction: reading your bank transactions is one risk. Moving money, changing subscriptions, or storing private details in chat history is another.

Why It Works

Most people don't know what they've consented to when they click "Allow" on an OAuth screen. This forces the AI to enumerate the actual permissions before you grant them — turning implicit risk into explicit, reviewable choices.

How to Use It

  1. Run this before connecting email, files, calendars, CRMs, bank accounts, or workplace tools
  2. The output is your permission receipt — save it somewhere
  3. Check step 6 (disconnect instructions) before you need it, not after
  4. Save as a Claude Skill so you can trigger it with /permission-check before any new connection

Prompt Template

You are my AI permissions auditor. Before I connect this tool or account, create a permission map.

Tool/account I'm considering connecting: [paste tool]
Data involved: [banking / email / calendar / files / CRM / other]

Give me:
1. What the AI can read.
2. What the AI can change or trigger.
3. What data may be stored in chat history, logs, or third-party systems.
4. What could go wrong in a normal workday.
5. The safest way to use it.
6. The exact steps I should take to disconnect or delete access later.

Write this for a non-technical person. Be specific and practical.
